We're still building PostHog. Twig is what happens when you realize AI coding tools need product analytics as much as developers do.

## The spark

It started with a simple observation: every time we used an AI coding tool, we found ourselves alt-tabbing to PostHog to understand what users were actually doing.

"Users are complaining about the checkout flow" → open PostHog → find the issue → copy context → paste into Claude → get a fix.

Why were these two steps separate?

## The hypothesis

What if your code editor understood your users? What if instead of asking "fix the checkout bug," you could ask "why are users abandoning checkout?" and get both the analysis and the fix?

## Why PostHog is building this

We've spent years building tools to understand user behavior. We know product analytics. We know session replay. We know what questions product teams ask.

Now we're applying that knowledge to the developer experience. Twig isn't a pivot—it's an extension. It's what happens when product analytics meets the AI coding revolution.

## What this means for PostHog

PostHog isn't going anywhere. In fact, Twig makes PostHog more valuable. The better developers understand their users, the more they need the tools to track and analyze that behavior.

Think of Twig as the interface layer on top of PostHog's data. It's how developers will interact with product analytics in an AI-first world.
