Every good product needs a story behind its name. Ours involves trees, branching, and a healthy disrespect for enterprise naming conventions.

## The obvious answer

Git uses trees and branches. We work with git. Twig is a small branch. Done.

## The slightly deeper answer

A twig is the smallest unit of a tree that can still grow. It's where new leaves appear. It's small, but it's where the action happens.

That's what we want Twig to be: the smallest, most focused tool that still has everything you need to build something that grows.

## What we didn't name it

We went through a lot of bad names:

- **CodePilot** — taken, also boring
- **DevBrain** — sounds like a zombie movie
- **Synapse** — too clinical
- **Forge** — too many forges already
- **Nova** — see above

## The real reason

Honestly? We liked how it sounded. It's short. It's memorable. It doesn't take itself too seriously.

Enterprise software has a naming problem. Everything is "Synergy" or "Nexus" or some Greek god. We wanted something simple.

A twig is just a twig. And Twig is just a tool that helps you write better code.
